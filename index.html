08 The Elephant in the Room Pre-Answered
"If your CAGR is 43.7%, why don't you just deploy your own capital?"
— The question every quant fund founder asks. Here's my honest answer.
REASON 01

Backtests ≠ Live Returns

I'm intellectually honest enough to know that a 43.7% CAGR in backtest doesn't mean 43.7% live. Slippage, market impact, liquidity constraints, fill rates — I've modeled some of these, but I haven't experienced them with real capital at scale. I'd rather learn execution in a controlled environment with proper risk guardrails than blow up my savings learning the hard way.

REASON 02

I've Seen What Happens Without Infra

In 2024, my brother and I built a BankNifty options algo. It worked — ₹50K to ₹3L in 3 months. Then SEBI banned weekly BankNifty options and our algo died overnight. We had no infrastructure to adapt — no alternative instruments, no hedging framework, no team to pivot the strategy. I learned that an edge without infrastructure is fragile. I don't want to be fragile again.

REASON 03

I Can Validate Edge, But Not Execute It

My strength is research: finding patterns, testing hypotheses, building systems. Execution is a different skill — order routing, managing live positions under stress, handling exchange outages. I want to pair my research with a team that has solved execution at scale.

REASON 04

I Lost My Team Once Before

When our BankNifty algo died, the people I worked with left. I was from a non-tech background and at that time I didn't have the coding skills to continue solo — AI tools like Claude and GPT didn't exist the way they do now. I rebuilt everything from scratch, alone. It took me a year. I know first-hand that building solo has a ceiling. I'm not looking for capital — I'm looking for a team.
